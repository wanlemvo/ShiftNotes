# USER EXPERIENCE

# Purpose

This document defines how ShiftNotes delivers value to its users and how it integrates into existing operational workflows.

The goal is not to redesign operations.

The goal is to reduce the amount of manual review required to understand what is happening operationally.

---

# Primary User

## Ted

Current Role:

Operations General Manager for Dsquared Hospitality at T-Mobile Headquarters.

Current Responsibilities:

* Oversee operations across kiosks and locations
* Monitor operational performance
* Identify inefficiencies
* Reduce waste
* Improve staffing effectiveness
* Improve operational consistency
* Make informed business decisions

Ted currently receives operational shift reports through JotForm-generated emails.

These reports contain valuable information but require manual review to identify patterns and trends.

---

# User Goal

Ted's goal is not to read reports.

Ted's goal is to understand what actions need to be taken.

ShiftNotes exists to reduce the gap between operational reporting and operational decision-making.

Instead of manually reviewing every report, Ted should receive a summary of the information that matters most.

---

# Core Experience

ShiftNotes should feel like an operational briefing.

Not a dashboard.

Not a chatbot.

Not another application that requires active management.

The ideal experience is similar to receiving a daily intelligence report.

Ted opens a single email and immediately understands:

* What happened
* What changed
* What requires attention
* What trends are emerging
* What actions may be worth investigating

Shift Lead

    ↓

    ↓
Intelligence Extraction
    ↓
Trend Detection

ShiftNotes reduces the amount of information Ted must manually review while preserving access to the original reports when deeper investigation is required.

Subject:

ShiftNotes Weekly Operations Summary

Food & Inventory

Guest Feedback

* "Bring back poke" appeared in 8 reports.

Staffing & Recognition

* Positive staffing observations correlated with smoother shift operations.

Operational Notes

* Most operational concerns occurred during closing shifts.

Potential Areas for Investigation

* Review recurring menu requests.
* Review food preparation levels during evening shifts.
* Investigate waste increases.

# Desired User Outcomes

After reading a ShiftNotes briefing, Ted should be able to:

* Understand operational performance quickly
* Identify areas requiring attention
* Detect recurring trends
* Reduce manual report review
* Make more informed decisions

---

# Experience Principles

## Passive First

Users should not need to search for value.

Important information should be delivered automatically.

---

## Preserve Existing Workflows

ShiftNotes should work alongside existing reporting systems.

The system should not require major operational changes.

---

## Actionable Intelligence

Insights should focus on helping leadership make decisions.

The goal is not more information.

The goal is better information.

---

## Drill Down When Necessary

ShiftNotes should summarize information first.

Original reports should remain available for deeper investigation when needed.

# Key Takeaway

ShiftNotes is designed to act as an operational intelligence layer that converts operational reporting into actionable briefings.

The system's purpose is not to replace existing workflows.

Its purpose is to help leadership spend less time reading reports and more time acting on the information contained within them.
