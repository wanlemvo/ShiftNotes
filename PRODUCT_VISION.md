# PRODUCT VISION

# Purpose

This document defines the purpose, philosophy, scope, and long-term direction of ShiftNotes.

While the README explains what ShiftNotes is, this document explains why it exists, what problems it aims to solve, and the principles that guide its development.

---

# Why ShiftNotes Exists

Organizations collect operational information every day.

In hospitality environments, shift leads routinely document

 Food quality observations
 Inventory shortages
 Waste metrics
 Guest feedback
 Employee recognition
 Operational concerns
 Process issues

The information exists.

The insights often do not.

As reports accumulate over time, valuable operational knowledge becomes fragmented across forms, emails, folders, spreadsheets, and individual memory.

ShiftNotes exists to transform operational reporting into operational intelligence.

---

# The Problem

Current reporting workflows are effective at collecting information but are not optimized for extracting long-term value from that information.

Typical workflow

```text
Shift Lead
    ↓
JotForm Submission
    ↓
Email Inbox
    ↓
Folder Organization
    ↓
Manual Review
    ↓
Operational Action
```

While this process captures important observations, identifying patterns across weeks or months of reports often requires manual effort.

Examples include

 Recurring food shortages
 Waste trends
 Frequently requested menu items
 Staffing concerns
 Operational bottlenecks
 Repeated guest complaints

Many of these insights remain hidden within historical reports.

---

# Vision Statement

ShiftNotes aims to become an operational intelligence layer that helps leadership identify patterns, generate insights, and make better decisions using information that is already being collected.

Rather than replacing existing workflows, ShiftNotes enhances them by converting operational observations into structured organizational knowledge.

---

# Design Philosophy

## Preserve Existing Workflows

ShiftNotes should work alongside existing reporting systems rather than forcing teams to completely change how they operate.

The goal is augmentation, not disruption.

---

## Reduce Information Loss

Important operational knowledge should not disappear into email archives or become dependent on individual memory.

Historical information should remain useful and accessible.

---

## Surface Actionable Insights

The system should prioritize information that supports operational decision-making.

Insights are more valuable than raw data.

---

## Minimize Additional Work

Operational teams are already busy.

Any intelligence gained from ShiftNotes should require little to no additional reporting burden.

---

## Build Incrementally

The system should begin by solving a small, clearly defined problem before expanding into more advanced capabilities.

---

# Intended Users

Initial users include

 Shift Leads
 Managers
 Operations Leadership

Future versions may support

 District Managers
 Multi-location Operations Teams
 Business Analysts

---

# Current Scope (Version 1)

The MVP focuses on passive operational intelligence.

### Inputs

 Shift reports
 Operational notes
 Daily observations

### Processing

 Data cleaning
 Categorization
 Trend detection
 Summary generation

### Outputs

 Operational summaries
 Trend reports
 Insight generation
 Leadership reporting

---

# Out of Scope (Version 1)

The following capabilities are intentionally excluded from the MVP

 Employee performance scoring
 Personnel recommendations
 Predictive scheduling
 Forecasting models
 Autonomous decision making
 Advanced RAG search
 Multi-location comparisons

These may be explored in future versions but are not required to validate the core concept.

---

# Success Criteria

Version 1 will be considered successful if it can

 Extract useful information from operational reports
 Identify recurring operational patterns
 Generate meaningful summaries
 Reduce manual review effort
 Surface insights that would otherwise remain hidden

---

# Long-Term Vision

Future versions of ShiftNotes may include

## Operational Memory

Searchable historical reporting data.

---

## Natural Language Querying

Questions such as

 What food items are most frequently requested
 What recurring operational concerns occurred this month
 Which issues appear most often during closing shifts

---

## Predictive Intelligence

 Waste forecasting
 Inventory forecasting
 Operational risk detection

---

## Multi-Location Insights

Comparing trends across multiple kiosks, stores, or operational environments.

---

## Automated Leadership Briefings

Weekly and monthly intelligence reports delivered automatically to leadership teams.

---

# Final Statement

ShiftNotes is not intended to replace managers, operational leaders, or existing reporting systems.

Its purpose is to enhance operational awareness by transforming information that already exists into intelligence that can be acted upon.

The long-term goal is simple

Transform operational reporting into better operational decisions.
